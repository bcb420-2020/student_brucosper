Were there expression values that were not unique for specific genes? How did you handle these?

Yes, as the experiment was performed with microarrays, there are several probesets that map to the same gene. This is expected, and although averaging or adding them may be done, following the suggestion of Obi Griffith in BioStars, we will leave them until later, in order to actually see the different probes during differential expression analysis, since its possible that we lose some significant data by trying to correct them at this stage (e.g.averaging could end up showing the gene as not differentially expressed, when in reality one of the probes is and the other is not).

Were there expression values that could not be mapped to current HUGO symbols?

Yes, a total of 8004, 4 probe IDs could not be mapped. From my research online this is common, and many of these are expected to be control probes. However, some of them may have been outdated annotations - but they are difficult to distinguish without going through the whole list, so I left them in place.

How many outliers were removed?

One sample was removed as an outlier since it was very far off in the MDS plot.

How did you handle replicates?

There was only 1 replicate for each condition per sample.

What is the final coverage of your dataset?

Originally we had 33297 probes, but after removing unmappable ones we have 25293.
